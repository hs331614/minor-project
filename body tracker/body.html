Body Workout

CHEST


  • Exercise: Barbell Bench Press
  • How to do it: Lie on a flat bench, grip the barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, lower the bar to your chest, and then push it back up.

BACK


  • Exercise: Pull-ups
  • How to do it: Hang from a bar with an overhand grip, engage your back muscles, and pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar.

SHOULDERS


  • Exercise: Overhead Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)
  • How to do it: Hold the barbell or dumbbells at shoulder height, press them overhead until your arms are fully extended, and then lower them back down.

BICEPS


  • Exercise: Barbell or Dumbbell Bicep Curls
  • How to do it: Hold the barbell or dumbbells with palms facing forward, curl the weight upward while keeping your elbows stationary, and then lower them back down.

TRICEPS


  • Exercise: Tricep Dips
  • How to do it: Position your hands on parallel bars or the edge of a stable chair, lower your body by bending your elbows, and then push yourself back up.

LEGS(QUADRICEPS, HAMSTRINGS, GLUTES)


  • Exercise: Squats
  • How to do it: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, and then stand back up.

ABDOMINALS


  • Exercise: Plank
  • How to do it: Assume a push-up position but rest on your forearms, engage your core muscles, and hold the position for as long as you can.

CALVES


  • Exercise: Standing Calf Raises
  • How to do it: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart on a raised surface, lift your heels by extending your ankles, and then lower them back down.

GLUTES


  • Exercise: Hip Thrusts
  • How to do it: Sit on the ground with your upper back against a bench, place a barbell across your hips, and thrust your hips upward until your thighs and torso are in line.

HAMSTRINGS


  • Exercise: Romanian Deadlifts
  • How to do it: Hold a barbell or dumbbells with an overhand grip in front of your thighs, hinge at your hips while keeping your back straight, and lower the weights until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings. Then, return to the starting position by driving your hips forward.

*Remember to perform each exercise with proper form and technique. Adjust the weights and repetitions according to your fitness level and goals. It's always a good idea to consult with a fitness professional for guidance, especially if you're new to exercise or have any specific concerns.
